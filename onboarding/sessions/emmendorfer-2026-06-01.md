## 01 · The financial engine — getting to the actual ROI math

You said you do 800-1,000 roofs in this market range and that hail + wind drives the siding spikes. Three layers I want to dig into:

1. **Walk me through a normal-year revenue split: roofing vs siding vs gutters.** I'd guess 70/20/10 in your market, but I want your number. This is going to determine which service pages we lead with — and which content cluster gets the deepest investment.

2. **The recent storm event drove siding demand. How does THAT year look compared to a normal year?** What's the multiplier on siding revenue when a hail event hits versus a quiet weather year? *We need to know how much of your revenue is opportunistic storm-chasing capacity versus durable baseline demand — it changes whether we build for steady SEO or storm-event spikes.*

3. **Of the work that comes in, what split is insurance-claim work versus cash-pay?** I'd guess 75/25 toward insurance given Franklin County's storm exposure. Confirm or correct.

4. **Average ticket by service line. Specific numbers — what's a typical roofing replacement, a typical siding job, a typical gutter run?** We use these to calculate the ROI receipt clients see weekly. The more accurate, the more credible our reporting becomes.

5. **Close rate. If you walk 100 roofs, how many sign?** John told me you've been burned three times by SEO. I'd guess your close rate is high on the leads that actually reach you — somewhere 30-40% — because the trust problem is at the top of the funnel, not the bottom. Confirm.

6. **What's the LTV of a customer?** Do they typically come back for additional services (gutters, siding) later, or is roofing a one-and-done for most? If they DO come back, what's the typical repeat cycle?

7. **What service breaks down to the highest margin per labor hour?** Your highest-volume service might not be your highest-margin service — we want to prioritize content around the latter.

## 02 · The services in detail — content authority depends on this

We're going to build service-page authority around what you actually do day-to-day. Skipping the "what services do you offer" question because we know it's roofing + siding + gutters. Going deeper:

8. **On roofing — what brand of shingle do you install most often?** IKO Dynasty? GAF Timberline? Owens Corning Duration? Once we know the manufacturer relationship, we can lead with the certifications (which AI search engines weigh heavily).

9. **What certifications do you actually hold today?** IKO ROOFPRO? GAF Master Elite? Owens Corning Platinum Preferred? Decra? DaVinci? *Every cert needs a verification number for the website — we'll need to capture each one.*

10. **For metal roofing — what profiles do you install? Standing seam in 22-gauge or 24-gauge?** Stone-coated steel like Decra? Exposed-fastener panels? This determines whether we go deep on "standing seam Union Missouri" as a content angle or whether metal is incidental to your business.

11. **On siding — what materials? Hardie, LP SmartSide, vinyl?** And specifically: are you HardiPlank-certified? That's another E-E-A-T anchor we can use if so.

12. **Tell me about a typical install timeline. Day one to completion for a 2,000 sq ft house roof.** One-day tear-off-and-replace? Two-day? Three-day? *Customers Google this question — having a specific answer ("we complete most full roof replacements in one day, including same-day cleanup") is a content goldmine.*

13. **What's your post-install warranty?** Workmanship years + manufacturer years. And do you actually back the manufacturer warranty paperwork — meaning if a shingle fails in year 12, do you handle the claim, or send the homeowner to the manufacturer?

14. **Gutters — seamless on-site? Aluminum, copper, what gauge?** K-style vs half-round split? Gutter guards as a separate upsell, or bundled?

15. **The signature techniques. The stuff almost no other roofer in Franklin County does to your standard.** Custom flashing? Welded penetration boots? Hand-cut chimney crickets? Skip-sheathing repair on the older Union homes? *This becomes the "Signature Specialties" section of the website. We need 2-4 things specific enough that competitors can't claim them with a straight face.*

## 03 · Matt — the founder bio

We're writing your About page. Everything below feeds the founder story. Specific over vague.

16. **Matt — what year did you actually start?** Not the company year, your personal trade entry. Age 18? 22? Where did you first learn to roof?

17. **Who taught you?** A father, an uncle, an early mentor? Three-and-a-half-decade careers in trade usually have a clear apprenticeship story — and that story is part of the brand if it's there.

18. **The recovery community connection John mentioned — is that something you're comfortable being public about?** If yes, it's an enormous trust signal in your market and unique positioning no competitor can fake. If no, we keep it off the record entirely. *Your call. Either is fine.*

19. **Tell me about the moment you decided to start Emmendorfer Exteriors.** What was the trade-off you were chasing — autonomy, quality control, family business, escape from a previous bad situation? The founding story becomes the brand spine.

20. **What's the ONE thing you do on every job that nobody else in the area does at your standard?** Be specific. Not "quality" — name the technique. Hand-folded flashings? Inspection at year 1 free? Personal walkthroughs?

21. **What was the worst job of your career?** Not for the website — for me, so I understand what you've seen. The lessons you took from it tend to become the things that separate you from a $5K agency's homepage copy.

22. **In 5 years, what does Emmendorfer Exteriors look like?** Tom + Amy running it day-to-day with you advising? Acquired? Multi-location? Still hands-on?

## 05 · The competitive map — drilling into RC, Frederick, A Roofing

We named three competitors from the May 27 call. Want your read on each.

28. **RC Roofing is #1 in your Map Pack grid and shares your old agency. From your perspective on the ground, what does RC actually do well operationally?** Pricing? Crew quality? Speed? Storm response? Brand recognition? Don't be modest — we need the honest answer to build the gap-closing playbook.

29. **What does RC do that you would NOT do, even to take their position?** Storm-chasing? Lowball pricing? Subcontracted crews? Knowing what you won't do tells us how to position you.

30. **Frederick and A Roofing — different agencies running them?** What's their pitch in the market? Do they outspend you on Google Ads, do they have a recognized brand, do they have legacy referral networks?

31. **What's the average bid spread when you compete against RC head-to-head on a $20,000 roof?** Are you $500 apart, $2,000 apart? Knowing the price gap determines how much we lean on value-positioning content versus price-justification content.

32. **Who's the competitor you'd LEAST want to take a contract from?** The one whose customers, when you do win them, end up being most painful? *We position you to NOT attract that customer.*

33. **Anybody coming UP locally — newer players who didn't make John's list but are growing?** Sometimes the biggest threat in 24 months is the company that's 18 months old today.

## 06 · The customer — who actually calls you, in detail

We need a vivid picture of the ideal Emmendorfer customer.

34. **Describe your absolute best-fit customer from the last 12 months. Not by name — by profile.** Age, household income, neighborhood type, were they storm-driven, did they shop around or come direct, what made them say yes?

35. **What does the LEAST profitable customer look like?** The job where you walked away saying "we should have priced higher or refused." *Once we know who they are, we write content that disqualifies them at the top of the funnel.*

36. **What's the #1 fear that walks in the door with every quote conversation?** Cost, time, storm-chaser scams, insurance claim getting denied, mess in the yard, contractor disappearing? Whichever it is, we address it on the home page in the first paragraph.

37. **When a customer is choosing between you and RC Roofing, what tips the scale?** Personal connection with Matt? John Casey's referral? Visible mentor reputation? Honest answer.

38. **What's the seasonal rhythm of your phone?** When does it go quiet (Dec-Feb maybe)? When does it explode (post-storm, May, August)? *We tune content cadence + GBP post frequency to your seasonal flow.*

39. **What's the geographic LTV pattern?** Is a Washington MO customer worth more long-term than a Pacific MO customer because of the repeat-business cycle?

The old agency told you nothing. We need to know what you actually know about your numbers.

42. **Who picks up the phone — and what's the response time?** Amy during business hours, an answering service after-hours, voicemail-to-text? *If a lead waits 4+ hours for a callback, that's a leak we'll fix Day 1.*

43. **Are you OK with us deploying WhatConverts call-tracking on both your numbers?** It records, transcribes, and attributes every call to the source (organic, paid, GBP, direct). Three-minute setup, free for you, gives us crystal-clear lead attribution.

44. **Are you OK with us pulling Stripe or your payment processor data into the dashboard later (read-only)?** So we can show you actual revenue, not estimated revenue.

45. **Last 12 months — roughly how many leads came in? How many closed?** Even rough numbers give us our baseline.

46. **What percentage of your business is referral?** Specifically referrals that came directly from someone telling another homeowner your name vs Google searches. *Helps us know how much SEO is replacing referral flow versus adding to it.*

## 10 · Brand voice — five adjectives + three forbiddens

59. **Five words that describe how Emmendorfer should SOUND on a page or in an email.** Examples: direct, honest, plainspoken, experienced, family-first. Give me YOUR five.

60. **Three words we should NEVER use about the business.** Examples: "innovative" (lazy), "premium" (overused), "passionate" (cliché). What feels fake to you?

61. **The voice question for the website: are we writing for a homeowner who knows nothing, or for an engineer / architect / informed buyer?** Most roofers write for "everyone" and end up sounding like a beige stock photo. We want to know who specifically.

62. **The Matt voice question: are you OK with the website having direct first-person quotes from you?** "I've been on Franklin County roofs since [year]. The first thing I check on every quote is..." That voice carries.
