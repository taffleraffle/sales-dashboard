# SOP · Jonathan · Discovery Call

The single most important call in the Launch Sprint. This is where the client's words become the source material for every page we build.

**Owner:** Jonathan
**Pipeline step:** 05 (see [RUNBOOK](../RUNBOOK.md))
**Length:** 60 minutes
**SLA:** Within 5 days of trial signup
**Tooling:** Fathom (recording), portal (questionnaire companion), Slack (notifications)
**Version:** 1.0

---

## Purpose in one sentence

Pull every piece of intel needed to build a 6-month content roadmap that reads like the client wrote it.

---

## Before the call · 30 minutes prior

1. **Open the diagnostic snapshot** in portal. Look for the 2-3 most striking findings to surface in the first 10 minutes of the call. Examples that work:
   - Impression cliff aligned to a Google update window
   - Top-traffic page is wildly off-brand for what the client actually wants to be known for
   - The buried hero page (real lead generator ranking position 20+)
   - Foreign traffic dilution
2. **Open the questionnaire companion** in a second tab. Resize so it's visible during the call without dominating the screen.
3. **Open the sales call transcript** (Fathom link in portal). Skim for things the client already said that you can skip. These are tagged "covered on sales call" in the questionnaire.
4. **Test Fathom recording.** Make a 10-second test capture, confirm it appears in your library. Fix any mic / camera issues now.
5. **Send the client the pre-call brief** if it hasn't auto-fired (24h before, templated). Confirms what you'll cover and why.

## The call · 60 minutes

### 0-5 min · Open and consent
- Warm greeting, weather, traffic, light. 90 seconds maximum.
- Confirm recording: "I'm recording this so our content team can build your roadmap directly from your words rather than from notes I try to take. Is that okay with you?" Wait for verbal yes on tape.
- Frame the call: "I'm going to ask you a lot of questions about your business, your patients, and the services. Some I already know the answer to from our sales call, so I'll skip those. The deeper your answers here, the more your final roadmap is going to feel like it came from inside your head, not from a generic SEO playbook."

### 5-15 min · Surface 2-3 striking findings
- Open the diagnostic on screen. Walk through the 2-3 findings you pre-selected.
- This is the credibility builder. The client hears that we've already done real work before they even started talking.
- Don't reveal the full diagnostic. That's the day-14 deliverable.
- Frame the findings as conversation starters: "Here's what we spotted in your Search Console. Does this match what you're seeing on your end?"

### 15-50 min · Run the 80 questions
- Open the questionnaire companion. Work through the 11 sections in order.
- **Skip rules:**
  - Questions tagged "covered on sales call" → skip unless something material changed
  - Questions the client clearly answers ahead of you → tick off, move on
  - Sections that are obviously not relevant to this client type → skip the section, note why
- **Do NOT type during the call.** The transcript handles capture. Your job is the conversation.
- **Where the client gives a generic answer, push once:**
  - "Can you give me an example of that?"
  - "Tell me about a specific patient where that happened."
  - "Give me the exact phrase they used."
  - "If you had to describe that in one sentence to someone outside healthcare, what would you say?"
- **Where the client dodges or doesn't know:**
  - Push once gently.
  - If still no, say "no worries, we'll fill that in later" and move on. Mark uncovered in portal post-call.
- **Pace:** average 25 seconds per question if you skip the answered ones. 80 questions ~ 33 min if you push on the deeper ones. Adjust to client's energy.

### 50-57 min · Summarise back
- Close the questionnaire. Look the client in the eye (camera).
- "Before we wrap, let me tell you what I heard so we make sure I got it right." Summarise the 3-5 biggest things in your own words:
  - The patient profile (who they actually treat)
  - The 1-2 services that drive the business
  - The 1-2 things they don't want to be associated with (Stacey: never "physical therapy")
  - The growth target (capacity-driven goal)
  - The single biggest worry they have about working with us
- Wait for them to correct anything. This is the moment the framing locks.

### 57-60 min · Next steps
- "Within the next 24 hours your transcript gets pasted into our system, and our content strategist starts building your 14-day Launch Sprint output. Day 14 you get the diagnostic + 6-month roadmap delivered to your portal, and we book a 30-minute walkthrough call."
- "In the meantime, Mersad on our team will reach out in your shared Slack channel around day 7 with a few things we're spotting as we go. So you're not in radio silence."
- "Any final questions before we hop off?"
- End on confidence, not paperwork.

## Handling common moments

| Client says | You do |
|-------------|--------|
| "I told you that already on the last call" | Apologise lightly, check the questionnaire mentally, move to the next unanswered question. Don't argue. |
| "I'm not sure" | Push once: "What's your gut?" If still nothing, "no worries, mark that one open, we can come back to it." |
| "Why do you need to know that?" | Honest one-liner: "It helps the content writer use your words instead of mine." Then ask the question again, slightly reframed. |
| "Can you just send me the diagnostic now?" | "I want to, but if I send it before you've told me what you actually want to be known for, it's just data with no direction. Twenty more minutes here and the day-14 version is built around you, not around the SERP." |
| "I don't want to share that" | Respect immediately. "Skip it." Mark sensitive in portal. Don't push twice. |
| Client goes deep on one answer and burns 10 min | Let them. The transcript captures gold. Skip more questions later if you need to claw back time. |
| Tech failure (Fathom drops, audio cuts) | Re-confirm recording on resume. If a 5+ min chunk is lost, schedule a 20-min follow-up rather than fake it. |

## After the call · within 4 hours

1. Open the Fathom transcript when it lands (usually within 30 min of call end).
2. Skim it once. Note any moments that need follow-up.
3. **Paste the full transcript** into the portal: `/clients/[slug]/discovery` → transcript box → submit.
4. The portal runs the AI extraction (~3-5 min). When it finishes, review the per-question answers. Edit the low-confidence ones. Mark "not answered" for any genuinely uncovered.
5. Click **Send to strategist** in portal. Auto-fires Slack notification to content strategist with all 5 inputs linked.
6. Mark step 06 complete in the runbook tracker.

## What good looks like

A discovery call you ran well will produce all of these:

- Recording is clean, audible, complete
- 70+ of the 80 questions have substantive answers (not "I don't know" or skipped without reason)
- The client said at least one thing that surprised you and that you couldn't have guessed from the data alone
- You ended the call with the client saying something like "wow, no one's ever asked me that" or "that was actually really useful"
- Your closing summary was confirmed by the client without major corrections
- Within 4 hours, the transcript is in portal and the strategist is notified

---

## Common mistakes to avoid

- **Typing during the call.** The recording handles capture. Typing creates dead air and signals you're not listening.
- **Reading questions verbatim.** Sounds robotic and breaks rapport. Internalise the question, ask it conversationally.
- **Letting the client run the agenda.** Friendly clients will derail you with their own questions. Politely park ("great question, let me come back to it at the end") and continue.
- **Skipping the closing summary.** This is the single highest-leverage minute of the entire call. Don't run out of time and drop it.
- **Promising deliverables not in scope.** If the client asks for something specific (e.g., paid ads management, custom dashboard), the answer is "let's see what the diagnostic shows and revisit at day 14". Never commit on the call.
- **Letting the diagnostic preview turn into the diagnostic delivery.** You showed 2-3 findings. That's it. The full diagnostic is day 14.

## Voice and tone (matches ROM brand)

- Direct, warm, declarative. Avoid hedging language ("perhaps", "I think we could").
- No em-dashes. No semicolons.
- Mirror the client's energy and casing. If they're casual, you're casual. If they're clinical, you're precise.
- Never use the word "physical therapy" unless the client uses it first (Stacey-specific, generalises: never describe a client's services using a term they've explicitly rejected).
- The phrases "your Launch Sprint" and "your Ranking Blueprint" are the canonical names. Use them.
