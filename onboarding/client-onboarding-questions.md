# Client Onboarding · Discovery Call Questions

**Purpose:** post-signup, pre-content-roadmap deep dive. Pair this with the sales call transcript. Goal is to pull every piece of intelligence we need to build a 14-day diagnostic + 6-month content roadmap that is elite-level, not generic.

**Format:** ~45-60 min call. Stacey-as-example annotations in italics where she already covered something on the May 24 sales call, so you don't waste the question.

**Rule of thumb:** the answers we get here are what separate an SEO agency from a strategic partner. Take notes verbatim. Quotes become headlines on the new pages.

---

## 01 · Business model and the commercial reality

These tell us what kind of patient is actually profitable, so the content targets that exact buyer.

1. Walk me through your packages and what each one costs. What does the typical patient spend in their first 90 days with you?
2. Cash-pay only, or do you provide superbills for out-of-network reimbursement? Patients ask this on the first call. We need it answered on the page.
3. What's the lifetime value of an average patient versus your best-fit patient? Where's the gap?
4. *Capacity question — Stacey covered: 70 visits/wk now, runway for ~130 in 6-12 months. Skip.*
5. If we filled your books to that 130, what would break first? Front-desk capacity, practitioner capacity, treatment room availability, billing? This tells us how fast we can pour fuel on.
6. What percentage of revenue comes from your top 1-2 services versus the rest? Pareto check.
7. What's the single most profitable patient persona you serve, regardless of service?

---

## 02 · The services, in detail

This is the most important section. Every answer becomes a service page. Don't accept short answers.

For each major service (Stacey: softwave, joint pain relief, functional medicine, weight loss, pelvic health):

8. What clinical conditions does this treat, by name? (e.g. softwave: plantar fasciitis, rotator cuff tendinopathy, lateral epicondylitis). Get the diagnoses, not body parts.
9. What does a typical course of treatment look like? How many sessions, over how long, how often?
10. What does the patient feel after session 1? Session 4? End of program? Sensory specifics win SEO.
11. What's your success rate honestly? What percentage of patients see the result they wanted?
12. What are the contraindications? Who is NOT a good fit? Honest disqualification builds trust and ranks.
13. What does it cost, and how does that compare to surgery / steroid injection / opioid management / insurance PT?
14. What technology or brand-name modalities do you use? (Stacey mentioned Softwave specifically. Brand names rank.)
15. What's the strongest single testimonial or outcome story you have for this service? Can we get permission to publish it with the patient's first name + before/after photos?

## 03 · The patient — who actually walks in

16. Walk me through the demographics: age range, gender split, income bracket, occupations.
17. What other healthcare providers do they typically see before finding you? Chiropractors, PTs, ortho surgeons, GPs, naturopaths?
18. What have they already tried that didn't work? List every failed treatment they tell you about in the first session.
19. What are they Googling at 2am the night before they book? Pain at night, can't sleep, surgery alternative, etc. These become the H1s.
20. What's the moment they decide "I have to do something about this"? The trigger event.
21. What objections do you handle on every consult call? "Will my insurance cover it?", "How long until I feel better?", "Is this just chiropractic?"
22. Why do patients leave a competitor and come to you? What was the competitor missing?
23. Who refers patients to you most often, person-to-person? Spouses, OBGYNs, personal trainers, mums-groups?

## 04 · Clinical authority — the E-E-A-T spine

We saw on the audit that Stacey personally ranks. Every service page going forward gets a named clinician byline. We need the source material.

24. *Credentials — Stacey covered: PT, MBA, MSN, RN. Confirm full list and license numbers visible on site.*
25. Who else treats patients at the clinic? Full names, credentials, photos, bios in their own words.
26. What's the founding story? Why did you start this clinic specifically?
27. What's your clinical philosophy in one sentence? "We treat the cause, not the symptom" is a starting point, dig deeper.
28. What methodology, training, certifications, or fellowships set you apart? (Mentioning the softwave is one. What else?)
29. Have you published anywhere? Pubmed, journals, conference talks, podcast guesting? *Stacey mentioned podcasts — get the list with links.*
30. Any awards, professional body memberships, board positions, teaching roles?
31. Has the practice been featured in local press, TV, podcasts? *Stacey mentioned TV interviews once a month — get the outlets.*

## 05 · Geography — the service area in granular detail

32. *Service radius — Stacey covered: most patients within 10 miles, stretches to 15-20 from TV. Skip.*
33. Which specific suburbs / neighborhoods / ZIP codes do most of your patients come from? Pull the top 8-10 by patient count from your CRM if possible.
34. Are there suburbs you'd love to attract patients from but currently don't? Affluent suburbs the brand should target?
35. Where do your TV interviews and live events happen geographically? These build local relevance.
36. Are there any neighborhoods or counties where competitors have locked down the search results? Need to know what we're walking into.
37. Do you serve any patients who travel from outside the metro for the softwave specifically? Destination-care content angle.

## 06 · Existing content + assets to mine

We don't start from zero. Inventory what already exists.

38. What podcasts has Stacey been on or hosted? *She mentioned a podcast — full URL list, episode titles, transcripts if available.*
39. Where are the existing articles she's written? On her site, on others' sites, guest posts?
40. Do you have professional photography of the clinic, the team, the treatments in action? If yes, get the Drive link. If no, schedule a photoshoot in week 1.
41. Do you have video testimonials? Recorded sessions where patients talk about results? Even phone footage helps.
42. Do you have written reviews / testimonials with patient permission to republish? We need real names, real outcomes, real photos.
43. Do you have any case studies with before-and-after measurements? Pain scale 8→2, range of motion data, weight lost, etc.
44. What does your intake form ask? Patients tell us their entire content brief on that form.
45. What's in your email nurture sequence today? Reuse the best pieces as articles.

## 07 · Competitors — who she actually loses to

47. *Generic agency mistake to avoid — Stacey burned $5k on physical therapy keyword targeting. Don't repeat. We hunt cash-pay, niche-pain competitors only.*
48. Name 3-5 local clinics you respect or watch. What do they do well? What do they do badly?
49. Name 3-5 local clinics that take patients you should be taking. What's their angle?
50. Who's the national or franchise brand pushing into Milwaukee? (e.g. The Joint Chiropractic, Airrosti, Joint Mechanix.) These set price expectations.
51. Do any competitors run obvious paid ads on your keywords? Worth knowing for the ad strategy if Premium tier.
52. Who has the strongest review profile in Milwaukee for shockwave / softwave specifically? That's the bar.

## 08 · Conversion mechanics

The roadmap is useless if traffic lands on a broken funnel.

53. Walk me through the journey from "patient finds your site" to "patient pays for treatment". Every step.
54. What's the primary CTA? Phone call, online booking, intake form, free consult?
55. What does the first interaction look like? Who answers the phone? What do they say? What's their script?
56. Do you have a free or low-priced entry offer? (Free 15-min consult, $99 trial session.) Stacey's softwave page should probably have one.
57. *CRM — Stacey covered: GoHighLevel partially set up. Confirm what's automated and what isn't.*
58. What's your show-up rate from booked appointment to butt-in-chair? Where do you lose people?
59. Are you running any paid ads currently? Facebook? Google? *Stacey mentioned Facebook leads — get spend, results, lead source breakdown.*
60. Do you have call tracking on the phone numbers? (We'd install WhatConverts in onboarding.)

## 09 · Compliance and what she CAN'T say

Medical content has guardrails. We need to know them before we write a word.

61. Are there any medical claims you're prohibited from making? Specific FDA / state-board language to avoid?
62. Does softwave have FDA clearance for the specific conditions you treat? Need the exact clearance language.
63. Are testimonials allowed in your state for medical providers? Some require disclaimers, some prohibit "before-and-after" framing.
64. Do you offer compounded weight loss meds (semaglutide, tirzepatide)? Different content rules apply.
65. Are there any conditions or services you offer but DON'T want to publicize for legal or insurance reasons?
66. Who reviews medical content before publish? Stacey, or a clinical second pair of eyes?
67. HIPAA: any patient stories or photos need signed releases. Do you have a template release form?

---

## 10 · Brand voice and the human

The new content has to sound like the clinic, not like an SEO agency.

68. How does Stacey naturally talk to patients in the room? Warm and educational, no-nonsense, motivational, scientific?
69. Pick 3 words you want patients to use about the practice after their first visit.
70. Pick 3 words you DON'T want associated with the brand. Common ones: "chiro", "PT", "alternative", "spa".
71. Is there a tagline you use today? If we kill it, what replaces it?
72. Show me 2-3 brand voices you admire (could be other clinics, could be entirely unrelated, like Patagonia or Notion). What about them resonates?
73. Do you want your face on every page, or do you prefer the team to be the front?
74. Are there topics you personally want to be known for over the next 24 months? Speaking, writing, podcasting. Content roadmap should feed your platform too.

## Notes for the AM running this call

- Record the call (with consent). The transcript becomes the source material for the content strategist.
- Don't read questions verbatim. Conversational tone. Adapt order to what she's already saying.
- Where she gives a generic answer, push once: "Tell me more about that specific patient" or "Give me the exact phrase they used".
- Where she contradicts the sales-call transcript, flag it gently and reconcile.
- At the end, summarise the 3-5 biggest things you heard back to her so she feels heard and we lock the framing.
- Brief Daniel after the call on anything that changes the deal structure (capacity ceilings, compliance constraints, decision-maker complications).
